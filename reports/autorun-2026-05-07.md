`[NEW HYPOTHESIS] 06:45:` Re-examining the 23281 fault pattern: `Memory access fault on address (nil)` + `Reason: Unknown` is consistent with a kernel writing to **the rocSHMEM symmetric heap past its end**, where the heap was never mapped. Looking at `/home/liyingli/rocSHMEM/src/envvar.cpp`:

```c++
const var<size_t> heap_size("HEAP_SIZE", "", 1L << 30);
```

The default `ROCSHMEM_HEAP_SIZE = 1 GiB`. For internode DeepEP at our scale (`ep_size=32`, `hidden=7168`, `seq=4096`, `num_experts=256`, bf16):
- Per-rank dispatch send buffer ≈ `num_max_tokens × num_ranks × hidden × 2B` ≈ `4096 × 32 × 7168 × 2` ≈ **1.75 GiB** alone.
- Plus combine buffer + per-RDMA-peer staging + per-team handles → easily 3-4 GiB needed.
- 1 GiB default would be exhausted on the first dispatch, and downstream kernels would write past the mapped end → `(nil)` fault.

`[ACT] 06:51:` Submitted **23283** (2N, battery-I-2N) and **23284** (4N, battery-I-4N), both with `_env_ROCSHMEM_HEAP_SIZE=4294967296` (4 GiB), keeping all other proven-good envs (`ROCSHMEM_BOOTSTRAP_TIMEOUT=60`, `ONE_GPU_PER_PROCESS=true`, `attention=dot_product`, `tgmm=True`, `deepep=True`, `bs=1`).
